# YES Bank Stock Price Prediction using Machine Learning

## Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the historical stock price data of YES Bank. The objective is to understand stock price behavior, identify trends, detect outliers, analyze correlations between stock variables, and generate meaningful business insights using data visualization techniques.

This project was completed as part of the Data Science with AI Internship at LABMENTIX.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze YES Bank's historical stock prices and discover patterns that can help investors and analysts understand market behavior and support better investment decisions.

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains historical stock prices of YES Bank including:

- Date
- Open Price
- High Price
- Low Price
- Closing Price

---

## Machine Learning

The project implements multiple regression algorithms including:

- Linear Regression
- Decision Tree Regressor
- Random Forest Regressor

The models were evaluated using:

- MAE
- MSE
- RMSE
- R² Score

Random Forest Regressor achieved the best performance and was saved as a deployment-ready model.

## Key Insights

- YES Bank experienced rapid stock price growth between 2015 and 2018.
- Stock prices declined sharply after 2018.
- Open, High, Low and Close prices are highly correlated.
- Price volatility increased during major market events.
- Seasonal variations are relatively small compared to yearly trends.
- Several outliers indicate periods of high market volatility.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Monitor periods of high volatility before making investment decisions.
- Use yearly trend analysis to understand long-term market direction.
- Combine historical stock analysis with financial and economic indicators for better forecasting.
- Use correlation insights for predictive modeling and risk assessment.

---

## Conclusion

This project successfully combines exploratory data analysis and machine learning to predict YES Bank stock closing prices. After analyzing historical trends and engineering relevant features, multiple regression models were trained and evaluated. The Random Forest Regressor produced the best predictive performance and was saved for future deployment. The project demonstrates the complete end-to-end data science workflow, from data exploration to deployment-ready model creation.
